Croissants

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled
  • 1/2 cup cold water
  • 1/2 cup cold milk
    •

    Instructions

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, salt, and yeast.
  • Cut the chilled butter into small cubes and add it to the flour mixture. Use a pastry cutter or fork to incorporate the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  • Make a well in the center of the mixture and pour in the cold water and milk. Stir until a rough dough forms.
  • Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5 minutes, or until smooth and elastic.
  • Shape the dough into a ball, cover it with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or until firm.
  • Roll the chilled dough out into a rectangle, about 1/4 inch thick. Fold the dough into thirds, like a letter, and then roll it out again into a rectangle.
  • Repeat the folding and rolling process two more times, chilling the dough for 30 minutes between each fold.
  • After the final fold, roll the dough out into a large rectangle, about 1/8 inch thick. Cut the dough into triangles.
  • Roll each triangle up tightly, starting from the wide end, to form a croissant shape.
  • Place the croissants on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, cover them loosely with plastic wrap, and let them rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours, or until doubled in size.
  • Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Brush the croissants with beaten egg and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown and flaky.
  • Allow the croissants to cool slightly before serving. Enjoy your homemade croissants!
