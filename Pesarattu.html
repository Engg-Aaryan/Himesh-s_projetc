PESARATTU

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup green gram (whole moong dal)
  • ¾ inch ginger
  • 1 to 2 green chilies
  • ¾ teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste
    •

    Soaking&Grinding

  • Clean and pick any stones from the green gram.
  • Rinse the green gram a few times until the water runs clear.
  • Soak the green gram in enough water for about 4 to 6 hours.
  • Rinse the soaked green gram well. In a blender, combine the soaked green gram, ginger, green chilies, cumin seeds, and salt.
  • Blend to a smooth batter. The consistency should be similar to dosa batter. If you’ve over-soaked the dal, add a little rice or flour to adjust.
  • The batter should be smooth and not too watery.

    • Cooking Pesarattu

  • Heat a griddle or pan until medium-hot.
  • If using a cast iron pan, drizzle a few drops of oil and rub with a sliced onion to season it.
  • Once the pan is hot, pour ½ to ¾ cup of the batter in the center.
  • Spread it thinly in a circular shape using the base of a ladle.
  • Drizzle ½ to ¾ teaspoon ghee on the edges and all over the pesarattu.
  • When the base firms up, scrape off lightly the thick parts with a wooden spatula, moving them to the thinner parts.
  • Cook until the underside of the pesarattu is golden brown and crispy.
