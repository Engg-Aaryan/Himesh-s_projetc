PANEER

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 200 grams paneer, cubed
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 green chili, slit
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 cloves
  • 2 cardamom pods
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • A few strands of saffron (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • 2 tablespoons chopped mint leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
    •

    Instructions

  • Heat ghee or oil in a heavy-bottomed pan. Add cumin seeds, cloves, cardamom pods, and cinnamon stick. Sauté until fragrant.
  • Add thinly sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and green chili. Sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy.
  • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, biryani masala, and salt. Mix well.
  • Add cubed paneer and sauté for a few minutes until paneer absorbs the flavors.
  • Add soaked basmati rice and mix gently to coat the rice with the masala.
  • Add yogurt, chopped coriander leaves, and mint leaves. Mix well.
  • Add water (1.5 to 2 cups depending on the rice) and bring it to a boil.
  • Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and cook for 15-20 minutes until the rice is cooked and the water is absorbed.
  • Once done, fluff up the biryani gently with a fork. Garnish with fried onions, chopped coriander leaves, and mint leaves.
  • Serve hot Paneer Biryani with raita or any curry of your choice.
