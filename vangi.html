VANGI BATH

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice (preferably short-grain or basmati)
  • 1 large brinjal (eggplant), cubed
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon vangi bath masala powder (store-bought or homemade)
  • Salt to taste
  • Few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • Lemon wedges for serving
    •

    Instructions

  • Cook rice in a pot or rice cooker according to package instructions and set aside.
  • Heat oil in a pan or kadhai over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds, and let them splutter.
  • Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  • Add slit green chilies and ginger-garlic paste. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy.
  • Add cubed brinjal and turmeric powder. Mix well and cook until the brinjal is partially cooked.
  • Add vangi bath masala powder and salt. Mix well to coat the brinjal evenly with the spices.
  • Add cooked rice to the pan and gently mix until the rice is well coated with the masala.
  • Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and curry leaves. Mix gently.
  • Serve hot with lemon wedges and raita.
