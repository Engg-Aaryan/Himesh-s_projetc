MUTTON BRIYANI

Ingredients:

  • 500g mutton, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup coriander leaves
  • 4 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • Whole spices: 2 bay leaves, 4 cloves, 4 green cardamoms, 2-inch cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • Fried onions for garnishing
    •

    Instructions

  • Marinate the mutton pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, biryani masala, salt, mint leaves, coriander leaves, and green chilies. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour.
  • In a large pot, heat ghee or oil and add the whole spices. Saute until fragrant.
  • Add the sliced onions and fry until golden brown.
  • Add the marinated mutton along with tomatoes and cook until the mutton is tender and the gravy is thickened.
  • In a separate pot, bring water to a boil and add soaked rice along with salt. Cook the rice until it is 70% done. Drain the water and set aside.
  • Layer the partially cooked rice over the mutton gravy in the pot. Sprinkle fried onions on top.
  • Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes until the rice is fully cooked and aromatic.
  • Once done, gently fluff the biryani with a fork and serve hot with raita or salad.
