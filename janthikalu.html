Janthikalu

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups chickpea flour (besan)
  • 2 tablespoons rice flour
  • 2 teaspoons cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ajwain (carom seeds)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for deep frying
    •

    Instructions

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine chickpea flour, rice flour, cumin seeds, ajwain, red chili powder, asafoetida, and salt.
  • Add water gradually and knead into a smooth dough. The dough should be stiff yet pliable.
  • Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadhai over medium heat.
  • Fill the Janthikalu maker with the dough and press it directly into the hot oil in circular or straight lines.
  • Fry the Janthikalu until they turn golden brown and crispy. Make sure to fry them in batches and do not overcrowd the pan.
  • Remove the fried Janthikalu using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.
  • Allow them to cool completely before storing in an airtight container.
  • Serve Janthikalu with tea or enjoy them as a snack anytime!
