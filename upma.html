UPMA

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup semolina (rava)
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon urad dal (split black gram)
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Curry leaves
  • 1-2 green chilies (chopped)
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 medium carrot (finely chopped, optional)
  • 1 tablespoon grated coconut (optional)
  • Lemon juice (optional)
    •

    Instructions:

  • 1.Roast the Semolina:
  • Heat 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee in a pan.
  • Add 1 cup of semolina (rava) and roast it on medium-low heat for 2-3 minutes, stirring continuously. The rava should turn aromatic and slightly golden. Set it aside.
  • 2.Tempering:
  • In the same pan,Add 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds and let them splutte, Add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds, 1 tablespoon of chana dal, and 1 tablespoon of urad dal. Sauté until the dals turn golden.
  • Add a pinch of asafoetida (hing) and some curry leaves.
  • Add 1-2 chopped green chilies and 1 finely chopped onion. Sauté until the onion turns translucent.
  • If using, add finely chopped carrots and sauté for a few minutes.
  • 3.Cook the Upma
  • Pour 2 cups of water into the pan. Add salt to taste and bring it to a boil.
  • Reduce the heat and slowly add the roasted semolina (rava) while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  • Cover and cook on low heat for about 5 minutes or until the upma thickens and the rava is cooked.
  • If desired, add grated coconut and a squeeze of lemon juice for extra flavor.
