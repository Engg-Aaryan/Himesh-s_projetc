PURI

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups whole wheat flour (360 grams)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon oil (optional, for crispiness)
  • Water (as needed)
  • Oil for frying
    •

    Instructions:

  • 1.Make The Dough
  • In a large bowl, mix together 3 cups whole wheat flour (360 grams), 1 teaspoon salt, and 1 teaspoon oil (optional).
  • Add just a bit of water at a time as you begin to form the dough, making sure not to oversaturate the mixture.
  • Knead the dough until you form a stiff, tight dough. It should not be soft like a roti or a bread loaf. You can add about ¾ to 1 cup of water in total.
  • Cover the bowl with a kitchen towel or plastic wrap, and let the dough rest for 20 to 30 minutes at room temperature.
  • 2.Form Puri:
  • When ready to use, divide the dough into about 30 small or 25 medium pieces, and roll each into a tight ball.
  • Lightly flatten the dough ball using the palm of your hand.
  • Apply a bit of oil to the entire surface. This helps the bread fry evenly and rise properly without the need to dust with flour first.
  • Roll the puri dough evenly into circles that aren’t too thick or too thin. Ideally, they should be about ¼ inch thick.
  • 3.Fry Puri:
  • Heat oil in a deep pan or kadhai on medium-high flame.
  • Gently place one puri in the oil, Press it lightly with a slotted spatula or spoon. It will immediately start to puff out.
  • Flip the puri and fry until it becomes golden brown on both sides.
